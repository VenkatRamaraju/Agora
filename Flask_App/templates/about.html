About Agora

What is Agora?

Agora, meaning "the marketplace" in ancient Greek, is a stock prediction tool built on the financial opinions of the Internet paired with numerical stock metrics used in traditional stock market analysis.

› Agora's Building Blocks

Agora's dataset consists of three main components:

  1. Conversations: Conversations scraped from Yahoo! Finance's "Conversations" tab for a given stock
  2. Headlines: Financial headlines scraped from 7 major news publications for a given stock
  3. Financial stock metrics: Numerical metrics that reflect the performance of a given stock

› Retail Investing

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, retail investing has become a type of revolution. As seen in January of 2021, retail investors were brought together on the Internet through forums like Yahoo! Finance Conversations and the WallStreetBets subreddit when they caught wind of the GameStop (GME) stock.

Individual investors believed that the stock was too undervalued and that GameStop's new leadership could revitalize the company back into a profitable business. These investors performed their own research on GME and discussed the stock on their platforms of choice.

Agora's reliance on these Yahoo! Finance "Conversations" aims to operate in scenarios like what happened with GME. At the time, many financial analysts continued to be bearish on GME due to the number of shares that were shorted by financial instituations, but retail investors continued to support the stock.

On January 5th, 2021, the price of GME was $17.37. On January 27th, 2021, the stock closed at $347.51. Over those 22 days, financial analysts only recommend selling GME, but retail investors and news publications continued to provide their support for the stock. As of October 28th, 2021, GME is $173.51.

GME is one of the many stocks that has been wrongly predicted by traditional financial analysts. Agora aims to provide predictions on stock ratings even in unpredictable situations like this by providing an importance to the voice of the average retail investor.



How does Agora work?

› Sentiment Analysis & Machine Learning

Agora's functionality can be broken down into two main components:

  1. Sentiment Analysis: Polarities of the conversations and headlines are derived through VADER
  2. Machine Learning: A logistic regression machine learning model determines a prediction based on the generated polarities and a set of seven stock metrics

› Sentiment Analysis with VADER

Agora utilizes the Valence Aware Dictionary and Sentiment Reasoner (VADER) module, found in Python's Natural Language Toolkit (NLTK) package. VADER is arguably the most advanced sentiment analysis library due to its ability to differentiate modern language paradigms such as slang, emoticons, and letter capitalizations.

After scraping the conversations and headlines, we utilize VADER to parse each of these individual texts to determine a polarity score. To determine a polarity score, VADER references a lexicon, or dictionary, that contains words paired to a negative or positive polarity. These individual word polarities were determined by outsourcing reviews of words through Amazon Mechanical Turk. VADER also provides the functionality to import a custom lexicon, which we leveraged to fine-tune the polarity scores towards financial terms. This financial lexicon was provided by Tim Loughran and Bill McDonald from the University of Notre Dame.

To truly differentiate conversations and headlines that conveyed positive or negative sentiment through financial terms, we increased the polarity of each of the words in the Loughran-McDonald lexicon (LML) by a factor of two. This way, VADER knows that these words contribute more towards an overall phrase's polarity.

› Machine Learning

Agora's knowledge base and prediction system is built on top of a Logistic Regression machine learning model. We tested numerous models, and found that the best accuracy was provided by a logistic regression model.

Logistic regression performs binary analysis and prediction, meaning for a given set of feature values, there can only be one of two outcomes. The two categories we divide our predictions into are "Buy" and "Not Buy". A "Buy" prediction by Agora is similar to a "Buy" or a "Strong Buy" prediction by a financial analyst. A "Not Buy" prediction is similar to a "Hold", "Sell", or "Strong Sell" prediction by a financial analyst.

Finally, this logistic regression model is trained on all stocks that have a sufficient number of conversation and headline data points generated, along with 7 numerical stock metrics. The stock metrics were derived after performing the Recursive Feature Elimination algorithm to determine which features provide the best accuracy from the model when paired with the Headline and Conversation polarity scores. The 9 total features are listed below:

  • Conversation Polarity
  • Headline Polarity
  • Beta: The risk and volatility of a stock compared to the rest of the market
  • Profit Margins: The ratio between the profit a company achieves and the number of sales achieved in the same quarter
  • Forward EPS: It involves making an estimation of a company’s upcoming earnings
  • Book Value: The value of all the assets of a corporation minus any liabilities (expenses or debts) held by the corporation
  • Institutional Ownership Percentage: Percentage of shares outstanding that are owned by financial institutions
  • Short Ratio: Calculated by dividing ht number of shares sold short by the average daily trading volume
  • Short Percent of Float: The short percentage of float is the percentage of a company's stock that has been shorted by institutional traders, compared to the number of shares of a company's stock that are available to the public

The model is tested against financial analyst predictions provided by the NASDAQ Stock Screener. These predictions are outputted to a SQLite database, and are offered to you, the user, through our front-end UI!



Polarity Scores

Polarity scores are numerical representations of the sentiment of a given phrase, sentence, or even paragraph. Sentiment, or the opinion towards a situation or event, can be considered to be either positive or negative. VADER utilizes its lexicon to determine the polarity score of the provided phrase. VADER generates four individual scores:

  • Positive
  • Neutral
  • Negative
  • Compound

The Compound score is a summation of the Positive, Neutral, and Negative scores normalized to be within the -1 and 1 numerical range.
